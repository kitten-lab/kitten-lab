# Detective Mystery Slot

#WIP

**Constraint**

`1 free spin feature or 1 hold-and-win feature`

**Desire**
`Create a slot with a self-feeding narrative loop.`


**Theme approach
`Classic detective-for-hire`
	base game  
	↓  
	client arrives  
	↓  
	free spins = learning the case  
	↓  
	new reel environment (investigation)  
	↓  
	trigger again  
	↓  
	free spins = solving the case  
	↓  
	return to office

That’s a **two-act investigation arc**.

1️⃣ anticipation  
2️⃣ resolution

Each beat becomes a series of **story phases**.

Classic detective in the office set up

Base game is reels over the detective's office space. We can see the office  door closed with the inverted detective gold label over the glass. The atmosphere is dark, the glass itself seems tinted.

We do not see the detective because **they are you**.

**Main game loop:** Waiting for a new case
Symbol set is generic theme to classic detective aesthetic and time period.

## You also solved the narrative reset problem

Many narrative slots fail because they don’t explain **why the story resets**.

Your explanation is elegant:

Detective office  
waiting for next client

So every reset is:

**“a new case.”**

That’s basically the same trick used by:

- detective shows
    
- medical dramas
    
- police procedurals
    

Each episode = new problem.

## Your environmental change idea is especially good

You wrote:

> the look of the environment is unique to the case and the symbol pack is changed

That’s extremely effective because it creates the illusion of **progress** without changing the math.

Structurally:

Office reels  
↓  
Case environment reels  
↓  
Resolution event  
↓  
Office reset

The player feels like they moved **somewhere**, even though mechanically it's the same reels.

That’s classic pseudo-progression design.
