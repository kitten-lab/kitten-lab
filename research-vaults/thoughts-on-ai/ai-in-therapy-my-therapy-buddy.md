# AI in Therapy - Concept: My Therapy Buddy

Some people need more than 1 hour a week of therapy.

	1 hour session  
	167 hours without support

Therapy is costly.

	$165-$200 per session out of pocket
	4 sessions>$600 a month of weekly sessions

Therapists are humans with real lives who cannot be available constantly.

Some people need help feeling safe to be social and communal after isolating or traumatic events.

The buddy acts as:
- journal partner
- pattern reflector
- crisis escalation channel
- role-play facilitator
- memory holder

Therapy Buddy is here to help!

- assigned to you by your therapist
- managed by therapist and under regulation to all HIPAA requirements
- an available companion for patients needing extra time and space on their therapeutic journey

therapist can:
- control the tone and constraints of each buddy
- insert homework for the patient, for example "journal nightly this week"
- review summaries of the conversations had with the buddy between sessions ahead of sessions
- record new findings

therapy buddy:
- works on homework with you (gratitude journal, try new things, write about event) between sessions
- available for between session crisis management
- contains patient history from both in session and out of session
- show related symbolic and historic patterns to reveal underlying traumas indirectly
- escalate to therapist or other appropriate-for-patient crisis management when needed
- create **clearly defined** role-play situations with the patient to repair old traumatic occurrences
- increase self-worth and self-actualization through witnessing

Therapist and Therapy Buddy act together for the patient's treatment plan.

## How your model works conceptually

### Layer 1 — Immediate emotional support

**Therapy Buddy**

Handles the _moment_.

Example:

Patient: I feel abandoned again.

The buddy might respond with:

- grounding prompts
    
- reflective listening
    
- journaling encouragement
    
- reminding the patient of coping tools
    

But it **doesn't diagnose or analyze trauma**.

---

### Layer 2 — Pattern detection

The AI quietly tracks patterns across time.

For example it might internally detect:

theme: abandonment  
frequency: 4 mentions this week  
related memories: teacher story, breakup story

But instead of confronting the patient with conclusions, it simply creates a **flag for the therapist**.

---

### Layer 3 — Human therapeutic interpretation

The therapist receives something like:

Pattern detected: abandonment-related distress  
Related references:  
• breakup conversation (Mon)  
• workplace feedback story (Wed)  
• childhood teacher story (last week)

Now the therapist can safely explore that **in session**, where the patient has professional support.

---

## Why this division is smart

Your structure avoids a big problem with many AI therapy concepts.

A lot of them try to make the AI do this:

interpret trauma  
provide therapy  
guide treatment

Which is risky.

Your version instead uses AI for what it’s **good at**:

- memory across long timelines
    
- pattern recognition
    
- constant availability
    

And leaves the **clinical meaning-making** to the therapist.

---

## There’s another powerful piece in your idea

You wrote earlier that the buddy could:

> “contain patient history from both in session and out of session.”

That creates something therapy often lacks:

longitudinal emotional memory

Therapists typically rely on:

- session notes
    
- patient recall
    

But a system like this could maintain **temporal continuity**.
